MtG Tourney Day 1 Recap: Notes from the gathering... Jun. 2015

Fans got their first taste of Constructed play at Magic World Cup XXVII, with Day 1 closing out with two rounds of Standard play. The eight rounds of Day One competition showed everyone in the world championship was capable of staring down even the best of the best in competitive Magic without batting an eyelash. The first event at Arena Mythic Championships was the Draft, which was touted as the highest-stakes Limited event in Magic history.

The broadcast team was still like they were at Arena Mythic Championships, and all the announcers did an excellent job, as usual. PiotrKanister Glogowski started the Magic World Championship XXVI with an aggressive Red/Black deck featuring two copies of Tectonic Giant and an Ox of Agonas. Six of the best Magic players around showed up in that specific build, four of them making it into the second day, two making it to Top 8.

A handful of Mirror Matches did distort the win total, but it was clear that Grixis and the tournament favorites, Izzet Epiphany, were the decks to beat heading into Day 2. Team Handshake teammates Nathan Steuer and Julian Willman sit at 7-1 on the ladder. The format is designed for four players, with one player taking on the role of Archenemy, while the other three players build a team to fight against the Archenemy.
